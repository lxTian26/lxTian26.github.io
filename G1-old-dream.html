Back Game - The old dream of a Fool

A storytelling based first-person exploration RPG created through the Unity engine. Players can spend around 10 minutes immersive experiencing 3D puzzle rooms to decrypt and try to escape a dream world made of obsessions.


The game got 83 points for Production Quality in the Assessment.

Why this?

Due to my personal passion for Chinese opera, I chose to use story elements related to it, including the theatre stage and the decorative model. This game is inspired by some of the escape mechanisms I have personally experienced and the film Farewell My Concubine (Chen, 1993). There are also some similarities between the protagonists of this game and the film; both are the "insane" ones who would do anything for the opera. The film also clearly shows the impact of the Cultural Revolution on opera, as well as the oppression of people and their obsession with opera. This is also the reason why I wanted to relate to this concept.

The game chooses the period of the Cultural Revolution as the background of the story, not only want to pay tribute to the film Farewell My Concubine, but also hope that more people can be aware of the protection of traditional culture in this atmosphere can not be repeated, so that more people understand the charm of the opera and pay attention to the inheritance of Chinese Tranditional Opera.

What is Cultural Revolution?

The Cultural Revolution took place in China from 1966 to 1976. Many artists and opera practitioners were labelled traitors to the country by the public simply for performing Chinese opera, which was interpreted as an old superstition from the Qing Dynasty. On the other hand, because of the communist propaganda for the working class, there was a great deal of stereotyping of opera workers who used to "specialise" in "serving" the rich. Numerous artists thus endured brutal "criticism" and physical and mental torture. Many of them chose to abandon their lives because they could not bear the suffering.

The story of the game's protagonist takes place during this period of darkness for the theatre industry. Therefore, some negative words and emotions will be seen in the plot of the game.

Reflection

The duration of production was limited to only one month, so the details of many scenes are not perfect enough, and there is potential for further improvement in environmental fidelity, and the environmental fidelity could be further improved. The difficulty of the mechanics is relatively low, and is more of a storytelling experience for the player. The links between scenes are a bit abrupt, perhaps by removing the full-screen UI to enhance the player's 3d immersion. the texture of the 3D animation and the coherence of the 2D animation also needs to be improved. In addition, the location of files in the Unity folder should be better planned from the beginning to avoid having too many files that are hard to find quickly.

However, the experience of making a complete game independently allowed me to actually feel the pleasure of making a game from scratch, including building the model, painting and colouring it, adding appropriate animations, lighting and sound effects. The most challenging part of pre-production was the creation and rendering of the models and animation videos, which took almost a week to complete. I also became more aware of how difficult it is to tell a story through only graphics.

More over, learning how to use new modelling software and shading methods took for a lot of time and effort, but it was still very rewarding to see the lighting, sound effects, models and materials in the final result that add to the environment. In addition, the gameplay process requires a lot of practice and experimentation, and this process is also very time-consuming. For example, the placement and visibility considerations of each item, the order of interaction of objects and the order of lighting cues, all of these require me to always consider things from the player's point of view.
