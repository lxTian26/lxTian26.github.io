Back The King's Meadow

(Group Project)

The King' Meadow is an immersive digital experience in which users interact with the story of an ancient kingdom while exploring a whimsical forest. The main components of the digital experience is a website, an integrated Unity project, and the interactive narrative that acts as a personality quiz.

During the group work I acted as co-manager, I was responsible for time management and planning, pre-production, asset painting, web interface and presentation slide development.

Inspirations From?

The personality quiz element of the project was inspired by popular online personality quizzes such as those created on uQuiz or Buzzfeed. These online quizzes frequently go viral over social media, as they are a quick yet entertaining digital activity that can be easily shared with friends, and provides users with a unique interpretation of their personality.

Example of a question from the “Are you a Soldier, a Poet, or a King” uQuiz by nour.

The branching narrative itself was inspired by the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse in Christian scripture. The Four Horsemen symbolise the end of the world and although each of them has different interpretations, they are commonly known as Conquest, War, Famine, and Death.

The flowers were chosen based on research conducted into floriography, or the language of flowers. During the Victorian era, floriography was used as a means of communication in an emotionally repressed society. Various flowers and their colours were arranged to silently deliver messages between individuals, such as giving red roses to express love.

When selecting the flowers to use in the digital experience, a list from allflorists.co.uk was used as a reference, alongside a basic Google search to determine how commonly that specific definition is given in correlation to the specific flower. The key traits of the MBTI type pairings that made up the results were summarised and used to select a flower that symbolises these traits.

Personal Reflection

My role in this group project was co-manager, mainly responsible for managing time and progress and reminding the team members of their deadlines. Apart from this co-manager role, I also participated in the design team as well as the development team, working on the pre-production summary and research, layout design, graphic design, website layout design and production, and presentation slide layout design and production.

Our group has a relatively relaxed approach to managing tasks and team members, with no rigid assignments for each team member, just requests for everyone to actively contribute, share and discuss their ideas with each other. We meet with the tutor every Tuesday and hold a more detailed meeting afterwards to discuss each person's plans for the upcoming week and the details of their subsequent tasks. Through collaborating in this group, I have become more proficient in the use of software and platforms for working online, and have gained more confidence in team management and co-operation.
