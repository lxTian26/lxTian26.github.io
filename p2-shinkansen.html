Travel with Shinkansen Simulator


An application that designed to simulate the sight of a passenger sitting inside the Shinkansen Bullet Train attempts to compensate for the purely exhibitionist experience of the current National Railway Museum (York) by creating a Unity 3d virtual environment.

Why this?

Inspired by the touchescreen of information boards next to exhibitions at some museums, this app allows visitors to gain knowledge while having an environmental experience by adding some interesting information about the features of the Shinkansen and can be placed next to the train in a museum.

Through my observations at the National Railway Museum at York, I noticed that there are not many interesting information boards next to the trains on display that would attract the interest of visitors or some children who do not want to do boring reading. I therefore had the idea of creating a storytelling, interactive display panel to introduce the unique history and story of the train.

Is this project usable?


Self-Evaluation

Self-evaluation form is created based on Nielsen's heuristic evaluation.

It was found that the product was able to cover most of the project requirements, but that the software did not provide any guidance or introduction to the operation of the software, with a relative lack of help and documentation. Flexibility is also relatively lacking. However, almost all other requirements have been considered and covered.

Reflection

The program as an interactive information board targeted to be placed next to the museum exhibits succeeded in presenting information and features of the exhibits to the visitors, it succeeded in visualising the speed of the Shinkansen and in giving the visitors an immersive Shinkansen ride experience. It is also able to automatically return to the default start screen after the user has been unresponsive for longer than the time limit. The highly functional prototype also fully incorporates and demonstrates the functions and requirements planned during the pre-design process in relation to the functional and nun-functional requirements of the project.

Improvements

There are still some parts that need to be improved, for example the train information is still mostly textual, using more pictures instead of texts might appeal to a wider audience and be more understandable for children. The visuals need to be better considered in the previous design. And in the future, how to get a balance between these different points of view will be the direction in which the production of subsequent projects will strive.
